⚡ What Is Electricity

The invisible medium behind every sonic gesture

Electricity is the movement of charge — a restless flow of electrons seeking balance. It’s not just power; it’s potential. From the hum of a modular synth to the flicker of a filament, electricity animates the inert, connects the isolated, and transforms silence into signal.

🧠 At Its Core

  • Atoms are made of protons (+), neutrons (neutral), and electrons (−).
  • Electrons can move between atoms, especially in conductive materials like copper.
  • This movement — a flow of charge — is what we call electric current.

There are two main types:

  • Direct Current (DC): Electrons flow in one direction. Used in batteries, synth modules, and most low-voltage electronics.
  • Alternating Current (AC): Electrons oscillate back and forth. Used in mains power and audio signals.

🔌 Voltage, Current, Resistance

Concept What It Means Analogy
Voltage (V) Electrical pressure or potential Like water pressure in a pipe
Current (I) Flow rate of electrons How much water flows
Resistance (R) Opposition to flow A narrow pipe slows the water

Ohm’s Law: V = I × R

🎛️ Electricity in Synthesis

In modular synthesis, electricity becomes expressive:

  • Control voltages (CV) shape pitch, filter cutoff, and modulation.
  • Gate and trigger signals define rhythm and timing.
  • Power rails distribute energy across modules, each drawing what it needs.

Electricity here isn’t just functional — it’s compositional.

🌍 Why It Matters

Understanding electricity isn’t just for engineers. It’s for anyone who wants to:

  • Build, repair, or modify gear
  • Troubleshoot signal paths
  • Design circuits that behave with intention

Electricity is the medium. Your ideas are the message.
